A single explicit formula that drives open-ended evolution in artificial life — and reads back a physical signature of living matter the metric was never optimized for.

Three artificial-life substrates · one recipe

TL;DR

Open-ended evolution in artificial life is typically driven by uninterpretable, black-box neural-network complexity metrics, leaving life-like systems disconnected from physical theories of complexity. We introduce MSPD (Multi-Scale Path Divergence, $D_P$) — a renormalization-group-inspired scalar that quantifies the temporal multiscale organization of heterogeneity in local transition laws. MSPD is an explicit formula that plays a dual role: as a gradient-free fitness function and as a post-hoc analytical lens on any simulation that exposes local transition laws.

Three claims, retained for this version of the paper:

  • MSPD-optimized parameters produce higher held-out complexity scores than matched random parameters from the same substrate (C1).
  • High-$\Delta H$ states correspond to states with higher instability to external interventions — the metric tracks the biology of the dynamics, not noise (C2).
  • Higher MSPD corresponds to stronger scale-dependent frustration, linking MSPD post-hoc to the frustration criterion of biological complexity (Wolf et al., Vanchurin et al.) without the metric being optimized for it (C5).
  • The protocol transfers across three substrates: Flow-Lenia, Life-like cellular automata, and Particle Life++ (C6).

Two nested loops

Open-ended evolution is a two-loop setup. An inner loop applies a fixed rule $\theta$ to states, generating a trajectory. An outer loop rescores the trajectory with some scalar — the "compass" — and mutates $\theta$. The compass is the bottleneck of the field: today, it's typically a neural network (ASAL, Leniabreeder), with no link to physical theories of complexity. MSPD is an explicit, RG-grounded alternative.

The metric

MSPD is a temporal extension of spatial Multi-Scale Structural Complexity (Bagrov et al. 2020). The RG architecture is inherited intact: the spatial coarse-graining scale $\lambda$ is replaced by an observation window $W$ on time, and cross-scale dissimilarity is computed in trajectory-distribution space rather than image space.

$$ D_P \;=\; \int \frac{\bigl\lVert \partial_{\log r}\, h_r \bigr\rVert^{2}}{\lVert h_r \rVert^{2} + \eta^{2}} \, dq(\log r) $$

where the heterogeneity trace $h$ measures, at every state $x$ along the trajectory, the average pairwise divergence between local transition laws at sites $i$ and $j$:

$$ h(x) \;=\; \iint_{\mathcal{I}\times\mathcal{I}} D\bigl(T_i(x),\, T_j(x)\bigr) \, m(di)\, m(dj) $$
$T_i(x)$ — local transition law at site $i$ in state $x$ $D$ — distance between transition laws (sliced-Wasserstein in practice) $r$ — temporal scale (window size)

From trajectory to scalar

Pipeline on real data

C1 · Optimization works

On Particle Life++, MSPD-optimized parameters produce higher held-out scores than matched random parameters from the same substrate: 7/7 matched groups positive, p = 0.008. Confirmed on Life-like CA by exhaustive rule sweep. Directional but underpowered on Flow-Lenia (6/9, p = 0.25) under the current optimization budget.

Sanity check: on synthetic controls, $D_P$ is zero for white noise, zero for a single drifting blob, and low for stationary role mixtures — it measures temporally organized heterogeneity, not motion magnitude or visual novelty.

C2 · Spikes mark branch points

A locally elevated $\Delta H$ marks states where a small perturbation sends the future down divergent paths. Quiet states heal. The metric responds to dynamics, not noise — confirmed within source trajectories (8/9 positive, pooled Spearman $r$ = 0.62).

C5 · Optimized systems develop frustration

What is frustration? Put three antiferromagnetic spins on a triangle. Each pair wants to point opposite — but the three constraints cannot all be satisfied at once. The system has many local-minimum configurations of nearly equal energy, separated by barriers. This is frustration — competing local rules with no globally consistent ground state — and the result is a rugged energy landscape where the basin a system ends up in depends on its early history, not just its current state. Wolf, Katsnelson & Koonin (2018) and Vanchurin et al. (2022) argue: living matter sits in this regime.

Operationalization · three matched rollouts per checkpoint

For each checkpoint $\theta$, run three matched rollouts: $A_\theta$ (same seed, free), $C_\theta$ (different seed, free), $W_\theta$ (same seed, early spatial constraint then released). The anchored frustration score subtracts ordinary seed-to-seed variability from the intervention effect, measured in CLIP-feature space:

$$ F_s(\theta) \;=\; d_s(A_\theta, W_\theta) \;-\; d_s(A_\theta, C_\theta) $$

MSPD-optimized checkpoints show larger $A \leftrightarrow W$ gaps than matched random controls — directional evidence on both substrates.

  • Flow-Lenia: 7/9 matched groups positive, sign-test $p = 0.090$
  • Particle Life++: 6/7 matched groups positive, sign-test $p = 0.063$

Neither result reaches conventional significance — read as underpowered evidence of history dependence rather than confirmed effect. The natural strengthening is replication with a larger matched-group ensemble, without methodological change.

C6 · One construction, three substrates

The MSPD construction applies uniformly across substrates that expose meaningful local transition laws — categorical for cellular automata, displacement for particle systems, tracer motions for Flow-Lenia. The recipe doesn't change.

Limitations and next steps

We are honest about what the current evidence supports and what it doesn't. The paper's §6 limitations cover within-window stationarity, statistical power on C1/C5, sensitivity of C5 to the intervention protocol, and the incomplete 3×3 claim-substrate matrix.

Three follow-up directions are deferred to the next version of this work:

  • Head-to-head vs NN-OEE drivers — run $D_P$-driven evolution against ASAL and Leniabreeder on a shared task, to test whether an interpretable formula matches the empirical performance of black-box judges.
  • Multi-objective with snapshot-MSSC — combine the temporal $D_P$ with the original spatial MSSC, since biological complexity is both temporally reorganizing and spatially hierarchical.
  • Scale-separation timescale $\tau^*$ — system-identify the characteristic time at which within-pattern dynamics separate from between-pattern evolution; the temporal analog of an RG fixed-point scale.
